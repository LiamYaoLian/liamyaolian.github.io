Work Ethics Checklist

Click each step to see more.

  • Step 1. Understand the Big Picture
    • Understand the goals and the problems of the team and the whole organization, stakeholders, environments, requirements, schedules, budgets, risks, etc.
    • Implicit assumptions

      Implicit assumptions in the requirements are the main causes of misunderstanding. Confirm and make stakeholders sign off to avoid misunderstandings and disputes.

      • How strategy consultants spot Implicit assumptions and define problems

        Check every keyword in the work requirements.

        • Example

          If we are required to "predict the sales generated by customers", there are several keywords :"predict", "sales", "customers", and one implicit keyword - "time".

          • "Predict": Do stakeholders have any requirement about how to predict?
          • "Sales": Should we use some accounting standards to define the sales?
          • "Customers": How do you define "customers"? For example, do we need to consider potential customers? Should we consider just a specific business line?
          • "Time": Which period does the question refer to?

    • Trap: taking initiative in the wrong way

      It is good to be proactive. However, taking initiative without seeing the big picture may lead to unsatisfactory results. This may sound obvious, but lots of junior employees fail to understand the big picture, especially when some expectations are implicit.

  • Step 2. Document take-aways after meetings and make stakeholders sign off
    • Purpose: Making stakeholders sign off will avoid misunderstandings and disputes in the future

  • Step 3. Confirm priority and deadlines
  • Step 4. Confirm file version, file naming conversion, and result presentation
    • Ensure one will work on the latest version. If necessary, add a suffix to the file name to avoid confusion. Move the file to appropriate folders timely.
    • Confirm the way to name the file. Sometimes we may assume others will understand the meaning of the file name, but it may not be the case.
    • Add explanations so that stakeholders will understand. Keep in mind that the stakeholders have different backgrounds and speak different "languages".
    • Making the work user-friendly: confirm the format we should use to present the result.

  • Step 5. Examine the file received immediately
    • Purpose: If the data we received has some problems, communicate timely will avoid delay and interrupting others frequently.

    • Check if we have all information necessary for the work

    • Clarify the meaning of data in the file and make stakeholders sign off

    • Check data quality issues

      See: https://liamyaolian.github.io/articles/data_quality_checklist.html

  • Step 6. Proactively update progress, challenge, and several possible solutions every day
    • Proactively informing the supervisor of the progress will save them some work and energy.
    • If we face some challenges, do not put forward just the problem. Suggest some solutions and confirm. We are hired to solve problems.

  • Step 7. Double-check before submitting
    • Purpose: High-quality work without errors will win trust.

    • Checking whether the result makes sense in the business context will help spot the errors.
